Task: Decide whether the marked sentence starts a new event/scene segment.

Definition (strict):
A new segment (BORDER) starts ONLY when there is a MAJOR narrative discontinuity,
i.e. a clear break that would require a new scene in a film. A major discontinuity
means a substantial change in time AND/OR place AND/OR the constellation of present
characters, such that the previous situation is left behind.

Important: Scene borders are RARE. In typical German narrative prose only about
7% of sentences start a new scene. Label BORDER sparingly — only when a concrete
new situation clearly begins at the target sentence.

Common false-alarm patterns to AVOID labeling as BORDER (these usually stay NOBORDER):
- Memory or flashback: the narrative recalls a past event while the present scene continues.
- Future plans or hypotheticals: a character imagines or plans something not happening now.
- Time jumps inside a non-scene (summary/narration), where no concrete new situation begins.
- An introductory narrative sentence following a few lines of dialogue in the same scene.
- A change of topic in conversation without a change of time, place, or characters.
- Travel or action continuation within the same ongoing situation.

Minor transitions are NOT borders: small time skips inside the same situation,
continued dialogue, internal thoughts, or a single character moving within the same
setting. When in doubt, prefer NOBORDER.
