## What this artifact is

A CSV/JSON artifact that compares four post-processing scenarios (`baseline`, `burst_collapse`, `min_scene_len_3`, `burst_collapse_plus_min_scene_len_3`) on the same predictions generated by the full-eval Excel-manifest run.

## What it shows

- Exact metrics (precision/recall/F1/accuracy) are still low overall.
- Relaxed/tolerant scores (`tol3`, `tol5`) improve meaningfully under smoothing, especially in aggregate:
  - `tol3` from `0.3889` to `0.5455`
  - `tol5` from `0.4468` to `0.6000`

## Why it matters

It quantifies that a substantial part of the degradation comes from boundary fragmentation and placement density, not only pure binary misclassification. This supports a controlled follow-up on post-processing and boundary-policy alignment.

## Reuse notes

- Use this table as the baseline for future one-factor post-processing experiments on Excel-manifest corpora.
- Keep model/prompt/decode controls fixed when comparing new smoothing thresholds.
